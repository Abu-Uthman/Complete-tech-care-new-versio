## AI Search Best Practices (CTC-Specific)

### Content Writing Style

**DO:**
- Start with direct answer to implied question
- Use natural language questions as headers
- Include specific numbers, locations, certifications
- Define service boundaries ("we do X, not Y")
- Write in first-person plural ("we provide")
- Use bullet points for scannability
- Add comparison tables where relevant

**DON'T:**
- Use marketing fluff without facts
- Hide key information below the fold
- Use vague language ("best," "leading," "premier")
- Assume AI knows industry jargon
- Skip geographic specificity
- Forget to define what you DON'T do

### Entity Optimization

**Clear Identity Statements:**
```markdown
‚ùå BAD: "Complete Tech Care provides IT services."
‚úÖ GOOD: "Complete Tech Care is a Melbourne-based on-site IT contractor
         specializing in smart-hands services for MSPs across 15+ regional
         Victoria locations."

‚ùå BAD: "We offer competitive pricing."
‚úÖ GOOD: "Our standard hourly rate is $95-$125, with retainer options
         starting at $1,800/month for predictable monthly costs."

‚ùå BAD: "We're experienced with retail equipment."
‚úÖ GOOD: "We are Coles and Woolworths inducted with NCR certification
         for POS terminals, self-checkout kiosks, and retail peripherals."
```

### Question-Format Headers

AI engines parse question-format headers more effectively:

```markdown
‚ùå GENERIC: "Regional Coverage"
‚úÖ AI-FRIENDLY: "What Areas in Regional Victoria Do You Service?"

‚ùå GENERIC: "Pricing Information"

‚ùå GENERIC: "Service Capabilities"

```

### Structured Data Priority

AI engines prioritize these schema types:

1. **FAQPage** - Highest citation rate for question queries
2. **HowTo** - Step-by-step processes AI confidently recommends
3. **Service** - Detailed service descriptions with pricing
4. **LocalBusiness** - Geographic coverage and contact info
5. **Organization** - Business identity and credentials
